NEXARA LABS — RISK REGISTER (TOP 6)
=====================================
Prepared by: Chief Risk Officer
Review cycle: Quarterly | Last updated: Q1 2026

RISK #1: Enterprise Customer Concentration
------------------------------------------
Category: Business / Revenue
Severity: HIGH
Probability: MEDIUM

Description:
As of Q1 2026, Nexara's top 10 customers account for 38% of total ARR. The two largest
customers (a Tier-1 automotive manufacturer and a regional US bank) each represent over
5% of ARR individually. A single non-renewal or significant downsell from either would
materially impact reported metrics and investor confidence ahead of any future liquidity
event.

Current Mitigations:
- Dedicated customer success managers assigned to all accounts >$500K ACV.
- Multi-year contract structures with 85% of top-20 clients (average 2.3-year term).
- Expansion playbooks targeting additional business units within existing accounts.
- Executive sponsor programme: CEO conducts quarterly business reviews with top-5 accounts.

Residual Risk: MEDIUM — Mitigations reduce but do not eliminate concentration exposure.

RISK #2: Model Quality and Hallucination in Customer Workflows
---------------------------------------------------------------
Category: Operational / Reputational
Severity: HIGH
Probability: MEDIUM-HIGH

Description:
NexaCore workflows often feed AI-generated outputs directly into downstream business
systems (ERP purchase orders, logistics routing, credit decisions). A hallucinated or
factually incorrect output that causes a client operational loss could trigger contract
penalties, regulatory scrutiny, and reputational damage. One incident in Q3 2024 (a
misclassified inventory record in a pilot workflow) almost cost a manufacturing client
renewal.

Current Mitigations:
- NexaGuard module provides pre-deployment bias scoring and runtime anomaly detection.
- All customer-facing workflows require a human-in-the-loop approval gate for actions
  above a configurable impact threshold.
- Nexara's SLA explicitly excludes AI output accuracy guarantees; liability is capped.
- Internal red-team exercises conducted quarterly to surface failure modes.

Residual Risk: MEDIUM-HIGH — As clients request lower human-in-the-loop thresholds,
exposure increases.

RISK #3: Regulatory and Compliance Uncertainty (EU AI Act)
-----------------------------------------------------------
Category: Regulatory
Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH
Probability: HIGH

Description:
The EU AI Act classifies several use cases within Nexara's target verticals (credit
decisioning, employee performance monitoring) as "high-risk AI systems" subject to
conformity assessments, mandatory audits, and potential prohibitions. Nexara has six
European clients onboarded and a stated goal of expanding into the EU market in FY2026.
Non-compliance could block market entry or require costly product rearchitecting.

Current Mitigations:
- NexaGuard was partially designed as a compliance response; roadmap includes CE-mark
  equivalent attestation for high-risk workflows by Q3 2026.
- External legal counsel (DLA Piper) engaged for ongoing EU AI Act interpretation.
- EU market expansion plans include a Brussels-based compliance officer hire (Q2 2026).

Residual Risk: MEDIUM — Timeline and enforcement interpretations remain fluid.

RISK #4: Talent Retention in Core AI/ML Engineering
----------------------------------------------------
Category: Operational / People
Severity: MEDIUM
Probability: MEDIUM

Description:
Nexara's competitive advantage is partly embodied in its 28-person AI/ML engineering
team. Attrition in this group — particularly the 6 senior researchers responsible for
the core NexaCore inference optimisation layer — would degrade product velocity and
could benefit competitors if talent is poached. Salary benchmarking shows Nexara is
10–15% below Big Tech compensation for equivalent roles.

Current Mitigations:
- Equity refresh programme introduced in Q4 2025 (4-year vesting, cliff at 1 year).
- "Innovation Fridays" (20% time) allow engineers to pursue research projects.
- Two co-authored academic papers published in 2025 to maintain research brand.
- Competing-offer match policy approved by the board for P5+ engineers.

Residual Risk: MEDIUM — Compensation gap remains structural; equity helps but is
illiquid until an exit event.

RISK #5: Cloud Infrastructure Cost Volatility
---------------------------------------------
Category: Financial / Operational
Severity: MEDIUM
Probability: MEDIUM

Description:
Approximately 62% of Nexara's COGS relates to cloud compute (primarily GPU instances
on AWS and Azure for inference). Spot instance prices for A100 and H100 GPUs have
fluctuated ±40% over the past 18 months. The FY2026 financial plan assumes stable GPU
pricing; a sustained 25% price increase would reduce EBITDA by approximately $1.8M and
push the company back to EBITDA breakeven.

Current Mitigations:
- Reserved instance commitments cover 70% of baseline inference load (1-year terms).
- Engineering roadmap includes model distillation work to reduce per-query compute cost
  by an estimated 30% (targeted for Q2 2026 delivery).
- Multi-cloud strategy under evaluation to reduce single-vendor dependency.

Residual Risk: MEDIUM — Reserved capacity reduces but does not eliminate spot exposure.

RISK #6: Competitive Displacement by Foundation Model Providers
--------------------------------------------------------------
Category: Strategic / Market
Severity: HIGH
Probability: MEDIUM

Description:
OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic are all expanding their enterprise platform offerings
(ChatGPT Enterprise, Vertex AI Agent Builder, Claude for Work). If these providers
build native low-code workflow tooling comparable to NexaCore, Nexara's core
differentiation could be commoditised. Early signals: Google announced a NexaCore-adjacent
feature in Vertex AI at Google Cloud Next 2025.

Current Mitigations:
- Nexara's moat is positioned as integrations (200+ pre-built connectors) and
  industry-specific templates, which foundation model providers have not replicated.
- NexaGuard compliance layer is a sticky differentiator for regulated industries.
- Evaluating a "bring-your-own-model" architecture (BYOM) to let clients plug in any
  foundation model, making Nexara model-agnostic.

Residual Risk: HIGH — Structural threat that cannot be fully mitigated; requires
continuous product differentiation investment.
