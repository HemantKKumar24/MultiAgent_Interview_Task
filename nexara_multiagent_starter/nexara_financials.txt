THREE-YEAR GROWTH NARRATIVE
----------------------------
FY2023 was a year of product consolidation following the Series B close. Professional
Services revenue was deliberately wound down as the company shifted focus to software
margins. NexaCore ARR grew 42% that year, driven primarily by expansion in mid-market
manufacturing clients in the US Midwest.

FY2024 saw the launch of NexaInsight, which accelerated upsell into the existing customer
base. Net Revenue Retention crossed 110% for the first time, indicating that expansion
revenue was outpacing churn. Headcount grew 40% as the company doubled its go-to-market
team in preparation for the Series C.

FY2025 marked the first EBITDA-positive full year. The company attributes this to improved
gross margins from cloud infrastructure optimisation (migrated 70% of inference workloads
to reserved capacity) and the high-margin NexaGuard attach rate. The board has set a target
of $75M ARR by end of FY2026, implying ~57% growth, which management considers achievable
but dependent on closing two large financial services contracts currently in late-stage
negotiation.

RISKS TO FINANCIAL PLAN
------------------------
- Two enterprise deals in pipeline (combined $4.2M ACV) represent significant concentration;
  loss of either would require downward revision of FY2026 guidance.
- NexaGuard revenue depends on EU AI Act enforcement timelines, which remain uncertain.
- Infrastructure cost assumptions in the FY2026 plan do not account for potential GPU
  shortages or price increases from cloud providers.
