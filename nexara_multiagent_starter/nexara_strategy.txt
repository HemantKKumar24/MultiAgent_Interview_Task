NEXARA LABS — 5-YEAR STRATEGIC PLAN (FY2026–FY2030)
=====================================================
Classification: Confidential — Board Approved Q4 2025
Presenter: Dr. Priya Anand, CEO

VISION FOR 2030
---------------
By the end of FY2030, Nexara Labs aims to be the market-leading AI operations platform
for mid-market enterprises globally, serving 1,500+ clients across North America, Europe,
and Asia-Pacific, with $400M+ in ARR and a clear path to public markets or strategic
acquisition at a valuation of $2.5B–$3.5B.

STRATEGIC PILLARS
-----------------

Pillar 1: Platform Depth (FY2026–FY2027)
  Nexara will invest heavily in extending NexaCore's agent orchestration capabilities.
  The roadmap includes native multi-agent coordination (agents that spawn sub-agents),
  persistent agent memory across workflow runs, and a versioned agent library that lets
  clients publish and subscribe to reusable agent templates. The goal is to increase
  average contract value from $187K to $280K ACV by end of FY2027 through upsell of
  these capabilities.

Pillar 2: Vertical Specialisation (FY2026–FY2028)
  Rather than remaining horizontal, Nexara will develop three industry-specific "solution
  stacks" built on top of NexaCore:
  - NexaSupply: AI workflows for manufacturing and supply chain (target: 150 new logos)
  - NexaCredit: AI-assisted credit decisioning for community banks and credit unions
    (target: 80 new logos; dependent on NexaGuard EU AI Act certification)
  - NexaOps: IT operations and incident response automation (target: 100 new logos)
  Each stack includes pre-built integrations, pre-trained domain models, and compliance
  documentation specific to its vertical.

Pillar 3: Geographic Expansion (FY2026–FY2028)
  Primary expansion markets in priority order:
  1. United Kingdom: Established regulatory familiarity (UK AI Safety Institute engaged),
     English-language GTM, and existing relationships with two UK-based PE firms as
     potential channel partners. Target: open London office Q2 2026.
  2. Germany/DACH: Large manufacturing base aligns with NexaSupply stack. Regulatory
     environment is demanding but navigable with NexaGuard. Target: Frankfurt presence
     Q1 2027.
  3. Singapore/APAC: Lighter-touch regulatory environment; growing demand for AI
     automation in financial services. Target: Singapore entity Q3 2027.
  Geographic expansion will be funded from existing cash reserves; no additional capital
  raise planned unless APAC expansion is accelerated.

Pillar 4: Ecosystem and Partnerships (FY2027–FY2029)
  Nexara will build a partner ecosystem of 50+ certified implementation partners by FY2028,
  reducing reliance on the internal Professional Services team and enabling the company to
  serve SMB clients (currently excluded due to high-touch onboarding cost) through
  partner-led delivery. Strategic technology alliances with at least two major cloud
  providers are planned to support co-sell agreements and marketplace listings.

Pillar 5: AI Research & Defensibility (ongoing)
  To defend against commoditisation by foundation model providers, Nexara will maintain a
  dedicated research team of 30+ engineers focused on: (a) model efficiency (reducing
  inference cost per workflow run), (b) domain-specific fine-tuning pipelines for
  NexaSupply and NexaCredit, and (c) evaluation frameworks for enterprise AI reliability
  that Nexara intends to publish as open standards, positioning itself as a thought leader.

M&A APPETITE
------------
Nexara's board has approved a framework for small-to-medium acquisitions in the range of
$5M–$40M, with the following target profiles:

- Data integration specialists: Companies with proprietary connectors to enterprise data
  systems (SAP, Oracle, Salesforce) that would expand NexaCore's integration library.
  Budget: up to $15M.

- Vertical AI companies: Early-stage companies with domain-specific models or datasets
  relevant to NexaSupply or NexaCredit. Budget: up to $25M. Two targets currently in
  early-stage diligence (names withheld from this document).

- Compliance technology: If NexaGuard's organic development timeline slips, an acqui-hire
  of a RegTech company with EU AI Act expertise is being explored. Budget: up to $20M.

Nexara will NOT pursue acquisitions of direct NexaCore competitors, as integration risk
is assessed as too high relative to the benefit. Inorganic revenue is not included in the
$400M ARR target.

KEY MILESTONES (FY2026)
-----------------------
Q1 2026: Ship multi-agent orchestration (NexaCore v3.0)
Q2 2026: Open London office; hire VP EMEA Sales
Q2 2026: Launch NexaSupply stack (beta, 10 design partners)
Q3 2026: NexaGuard EU AI Act pilot certification complete
Q4 2026: Close 2 large FS contracts currently in pipeline ($4.2M combined ACV)
Q4 2026: Achieve $75M ARR milestone

RISK TO STRATEGY
----------------
The primary strategic risk is that the vertical specialisation bets (Pillar 2) dilute
engineering focus and delay the platform depth work (Pillar 1). The board has approved a
"pillar sequencing" principle: Pillar 1 milestones must be on track before Pillar 2 work
may draw more than 30% of engineering capacity.
