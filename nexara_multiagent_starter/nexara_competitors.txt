NEXARA LABS — COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
=====================================
Prepared by: Head of Product Strategy
Last updated: Q1 2026
Source: Public filings, analyst reports, win/loss interviews with sales team

OVERVIEW
--------
Nexara Labs operates in the enterprise AI workflow automation market, which IDC estimates
at $8.4B in 2025, growing at a CAGR of 34% through 2028. Nexara competes primarily on
three dimensions: ease of deployment for non-ML teams, depth of enterprise integrations,
and compliance/governance features. The competitive set can be divided into (a) dedicated
AI workflow platforms, (b) legacy RPA vendors expanding into AI, and (c) foundation model
providers building enterprise platforms.

COMPETITOR 1: FlowMind AI (Primary Direct Competitor)
------------------------------------------------------
Founded: 2019 | HQ: San Francisco, CA | Stage: Series D ($210M raised)
Estimated ARR: $95M (per TechCrunch, Jan 2026)
Headcount: ~520

Positioning:
FlowMind AI is Nexara's most direct competitor. Both companies target mid-market
enterprises with low-code AI workflow platforms. FlowMind's go-to-market emphasises speed
of deployment ("live in 48 hours") and a larger pre-built template library (800+ templates
vs Nexara's 340+). FlowMind is ahead of Nexara in ARR but behind on gross margin (63%
vs Nexara's 71%), suggesting a less efficient cost structure.

Strengths vs Nexara:
- Larger sales team (85 AEs vs Nexara's 42); broader logo coverage in SMB segment.
- More extensive template library and a more mature partner ecosystem (120+ certified
  implementation partners).
- Earlier mover into APAC; Singapore office opened Q1 2025.

Weaknesses vs Nexara:
- No native compliance/governance layer equivalent to NexaGuard; positioned as a
  weakness in financial services deals.
- Higher churn (reported 8.2% gross logo churn vs Nexara's 4.1%); attributed to weaker
  customer success motion.
- Less sophisticated RAG capabilities; relies on out-of-the-box LLM context windows
  rather than purpose-built retrieval pipelines.

Win rate vs FlowMind (Nexara internal data): 54% (last 4 quarters, enterprise deals >$100K)

COMPETITOR 2: ProcessIQ (Legacy RPA Vendor)
--------------------------------------------
Founded: 2012 | HQ: Chicago, IL | Stage: Public (NASDAQ: PRIQ, ~$1.8B market cap)
Revenue: $340M (FY2025, total; AI-relevant segment ~$80M)
Headcount: ~2,100

Positioning:
ProcessIQ is a traditional RPA (robotic process automation) company that has been
aggressively rebranding as an "AI-first" platform since 2023. Its core product is deeply
embedded in large enterprise operations teams, and it is leveraging these relationships
to cross-sell AI workflow capabilities. ProcessIQ's AI layer is less technically
sophisticated than Nexara's but benefits from massive distribution and existing trust
with IT procurement teams.

Strengths vs Nexara:
- Installed base: 1,200+ enterprise clients who already trust ProcessIQ and are natural
  upsell targets for AI features; Nexara must displace or co-exist.
- Professional services depth: 600-person services org capable of handling complex
  enterprise change management that Nexara's 40-person team cannot match.
- Balance sheet: Public company with $180M cash; can absorb losses to win deals.

Weaknesses vs Nexara:
- Technical debt: ProcessIQ's AI layer is a thin wrapper over OpenAI APIs bolted onto
  a 10-year-old RPA architecture. Performance on complex multi-step workflows is poor.
- Slow product velocity: Public company bureaucracy; major features take 12–18 months
  to ship vs Nexara's 6–10 week release cycles.
- Customer perception: Existing clients view ProcessIQ as a legacy automation vendor,
  not an AI innovator; brand credibility is a documented friction point in AI deals.

Win rate vs ProcessIQ (Nexara internal data): 71% in greenfield deals; 22% in
ProcessIQ-incumbent accounts (displacement is very difficult).

COMPETITOR 3: Vertex Agents (Google Cloud)
-------------------------------------------
Parent: Alphabet / Google | Product: Vertex AI Agent Builder
Launch: GA announced Google Cloud Next 2025

Positioning:
Vertex Agents represents the most significant long-term strategic threat to Nexara. Google
is bringing enterprise agent workflow capabilities directly into Vertex AI, bundled with
Google Cloud infrastructure. The product targets the same persona (operations teams wanting
to build AI workflows) and is priced aggressively to drive Google Cloud consumption.
As of Q1 2026, Vertex Agents is in early-adopter phase and lacks the enterprise
integrations and compliance features of NexaCore, but Google's resources and distribution
make this a high-priority watch item.

Strengths vs Nexara:
- Distribution: Sold alongside GCP, giving Vertex Agents instant access to Google's
  enterprise account base.
- Pricing: Priced as a GCP service (usage-based), which is attractive to cost-conscious
  buyers and difficult for Nexara to compete with on pure price.
- Model access: Native integration with Gemini 1.5/2.0 provides best-in-class model
  quality for Google Cloud clients.

Weaknesses vs Nexara (current state):
- Integration depth: 34 connectors at launch vs Nexara's 200+. Enterprise deals require
  connectors to SAP, Oracle, and legacy ERPs that Vertex Agents does not yet support.
- No compliance layer: NexaGuard equivalent is on the Vertex AI roadmap but not shipped.
- Customisation: Vertex Agents is more opinionated and less configurable than NexaCore;
  complex enterprise workflows require workarounds.

Win rate vs Vertex Agents: Insufficient data (product too new); Nexara is tracking 6
competitive deals where Vertex Agents is being evaluated alongside NexaCore.
